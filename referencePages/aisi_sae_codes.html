AISI/SAE Steel Designation Codes

How to read and decode standard steel grade numbers

The AISI (American Iron and Steel Institute) and SAE (Society of Automotive Engineers) jointly developed a four-digit numbering system for carbon and alloy steels. The digits encode both the alloy type and the nominal carbon content, giving engineers a quick way to identify a steel's composition from its grade number alone.

How to Read a Four-Digit Grade

Each digit position carries specific information. The first two digits identify the alloy series (the primary and secondary alloying elements), while the last two digits give the carbon content in hundredths of a percent (points of carbon).

1
Alloy series (major element)
×
0
Alloy modifier or subgroup
×
4
Carbon content (hundredths %)
5

Example: 1045 — Series 10 (plain carbon), 0.45% C
Example: 4140 — Series 41 (Cr-Mo alloy), 0.40% C

Stainless Steel Series (AISI)

Stainless steels use a three-digit AISI system. The first digit indicates the series (austenitic, ferritic, or martensitic), and the last two digits distinguish grades within the series. Minimum 10.5% Cr is required for the passive oxide layer that provides corrosion resistance.

Series Crystal Structure Principal Alloying Elements Key Characteristics
2xx Austenitic (FCC) Cr (16–19%), Mn (5–10%), N — low Ni Non-magnetic, weldable, lower cost than 3xx
3xx Austenitic (FCC) Cr (16–26%), Ni (6–22%) Non-magnetic, excellent corrosion resistance, most common stainless
4xx Ferritic or Martensitic (BCC) Cr (10.5–18%), low Ni Magnetic; ferritic grades not hardenable, martensitic grades hardenable by heat treatment
5xx Martensitic (BCC) Cr (12%), higher C Heat-treatable, high strength, limited corrosion resistance
6xx Precipitation Hardening Cr, Ni + Cu or Al or Nb Very high strength via aging; 17-4 PH (630) most common

Letter Prefixes & Suffixes

Single letters placed before or after the four-digit code modify the grade designation to indicate a process, addition, or specification type.

E____
Electric furnace steel — tighter chemistry control, fewer inclusions
____B
Boron added (0.0005–0.003%) to increase hardenability
____L
Leaded (0.15–0.35% Pb) for improved machinability
____H
Hardenability band — chemistry adjusted to guarantee a Jominy hardenability range
____L (stainless)
Low carbon (≤ 0.03%) — reduces carbide precipitation during welding (sensitization)
____F
Free-machining version of a grade (added S or Pb)
