How Atomization Works

Atomization is the primary commercial route for making metal powder. A stream of molten metal is disintegrated into droplets by high-velocity fluid jets (water or gas). The droplets solidify in flight to form powder particles. The choice of atomizing medium — water or inert gas — is the single biggest factor controlling particle shape, surface chemistry, and production cost. Höganäs AB (Sweden), the world's largest iron powder producer, uses both routes depending on end application.

Irregular to angular; circularity ~0.6–0.75
Effect on Processing
High green strength (particle interlock). Poor flowability. High compressibility — good for die pressing. Oxide must be reduced during sintering.

Gas-Atomized (Spherical)

Surface Texture
Smooth, clean. Minimal oxide. Occasional small satellite spheres from secondary droplet collision.
Shape Descriptor (ISO 9276-6)
Spherical; circularity 0.92–0.99
Effect on Processing
Excellent flowability (critical for MIM feedstock and powder bed AM). High apparent density. Lower green strength — requires binder for MIM. Low oxygen content.

Powder Characterization Methods & Data

Höganäs specifies every commercial powder grade against a comprehensive characterization suite. The most important properties for PM processing are particle size distribution (PSD), apparent density (AD), tap density, Hall flow rate, green density, and green strength. Each is strongly influenced by the atomization method and any post-atomization treatments (annealing, classification, reduction).

Particle Size Distribution — Water vs. Gas Atomized Iron

Volume-weighted PSD (q₃) from laser diffraction. Water-atomized (Höganäs ASC 100.29): D10 ≈ 40 µm, D50 ≈ 100 µm, D90 ≈ 200 µm. Gas-atomized (Höganäs AM Fe): D10 ≈ 15 µm, D50 ≈ 35 µm, D90 ≈ 65 µm. Narrower PSD and finer size in gas-atomized powder is essential for fine feature resolution in AM.

Compressibility Curve — Green Density vs. Compaction Pressure

Compressibility is the most important die-pressing characteristic. Irregular water-atomized particles achieve higher green density at all pressures because they deform plastically and interlock. Spherical gas-atomized powder springs back elastically (Bauschinger effect), limiting green density.

Höganäs PM Design Guidelines

Höganäs publish design rules for PM components in their Handbook for Sintered Components. The rules reflect the fundamental constraint of uniaxial die compaction: features must be formable by punches moving along one axis and ejectable without damage. Green = preferred · Red = avoid
