Weld Metal Solidification Stages

Stage 1 — Liquid Weld Pool
The arc melts base metal and filler to form a liquid pool. Temperature at pool center can exceed 2000°C. The fusion boundary separates solid base metal from liquid weld metal.

HAZ Microstructure — Critical Zones

CGHAZ — Most Critical

Rapid grain growth occurs above ~1100°C as austenite grains are unpinned (carbides dissolve). Grains can exceed 200 µm. On cooling, the coarse prior-austenite grain (PAG) structure promotes upper bainite and Widmanstätten ferrite — or martensite if cooling is rapid.

PAG size>100 µm typical
Dominant phaseUpper bainite / martensite
CVN toughnessLowest in HAZ
HIC susceptible Poor sub-zero toughness Preheat critical

FGHAZ — Best Toughness

Heated just above Ac3 allows full austenitization without grain growth (carbides remain as pinning particles). Recrystallized fine grains (10–30 µm) form on cooling as fine ferrite, acicular ferrite, or lower bainite depending on hardenability.

PAG size10–30 µm
Dominant phaseFine ferrite / acicular ferrite
CVN toughnessEqual or better than base
Best HAZ region Multi-pass reheating risk

ICHAZ — MA Constituent Risk

Partial transformation between Ac1 and Ac3 leaves a two-phase (ferrite + austenite) structure at peak temperature. On cooling, the austenite reverts to martensite-austenite (MA) islands — hard brittle particles in a soft ferrite matrix that degrade toughness.

Peak temp rangeAc1–Ac3 (723–910°C)
Key constituentMA islands
CVN toughnessVariable — can be low
MA embrittlement Worst in multi-pass welds

HAZ Thermal Cycle Diagram

Each location in the HAZ experiences a unique thermal cycle — a rapid rise to a peak temperature followed by cooling. The peak temperature determines which zone the material falls into; the cooling rate (especially ∆t8/5) determines the resulting microstructure.
