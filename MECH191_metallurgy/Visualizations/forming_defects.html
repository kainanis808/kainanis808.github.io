Forming Defects — TWI Reference

These defects commonly arise during bulk and sheet forming. The interface of a lap or seam contains oxide scale preventing metallurgical bonding; delamination is fully internal. Early detection with appropriate NDT is critical before downstream machining or service.

Laps

SurfaceForgingRolling

Metal folds back onto itself during rolling or forging. The fold interface is oxide-filled, so no metallurgical bond forms. Appears as a tight, longitudinal surface crack nearly parallel to the surface — may only become visible after machining.

Causes: Excessive reduction per pass, flash fold-over in forging, guide misalignment.
Risk: Fatigue crack initiator; invisible until the surface material is removed.

Seams

SurfaceNear-surfaceRollingDrawing

Pre-existing defects (ingot pipe, subsurface blowholes, scabs) that elongate and open during rolling or drawing. Run longitudinally, oxide-filled, and typically narrow.

Causes: Ingot pipe, subsurface porosity, billet conditioning issues.
Risk: Can run deep; cause brittle fracture during further cold processing.

Delamination

InternalRollingForging

Internal separation along planes of weakness — planar non-metallic inclusion stringers or centerline segregation elongated during rolling. Crack is parallel to the rolling plane and invisible externally.

Causes: Sulfide/oxide stringers, macro-segregation, center porosity.
Risk: Severely degrades short-transverse properties; causes lamellar tearing in welds.

Additional Forming Defects

Internal Bursts (Chevron Cracking)

InternalExtrusionDrawing

Periodic V-shaped centerline cracks in drawn or extruded products. High die angle or excessive reduction creates tensile hydrostatic stress at the centerline, causing "cupping" failures in wire drawing.

Earring & Orange Peel

SurfaceSheet

Earring: Lobes at the top of a deep-drawn cup from planar anisotropy (Δr ≠ 0) due to crystallographic texture. Orange peel: Rough surface caused by large grain size deforming non-uniformly — worsens at strains above ~20%.

NDT Detection Methods

Choose based on defect location (surface vs. internal), material type (ferromagnetic vs. non-ferromagnetic), and production speed requirements.

Magnetic Particle (MT)

ASTM E1444 | Ferromagnetic only
Surface & near-surface to ~3 mm

Liquid Penetrant (PT)

ASTM E165 | All materials
Surface-open defects only

Ultrasonic (UT)

ASTM E317 | All materials
Internal defects; full volume

Eddy Current (ET)

ASTM E309 | Conductive materials
Surface & near-surface; high speed

Radiography (RT)

ASTM E94 | All materials
Internal volumetric defects

Mag. Flux Leakage (MFL)

API 5L | Ferromagnetic tube/pipe
High-speed inline inspection
