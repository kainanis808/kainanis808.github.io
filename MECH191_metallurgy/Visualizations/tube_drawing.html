Tube & Pipe – Manufacturing, Drawing & Inspection

Based on Tube & Pipe Association (TPA) International, ASTM, EN, and ASME standards

Tube & Pipe Manufacturing Processes

Mannesmann Rotary Piercing (Seamless)

Process: Heated round billet fed between two skewed barrel rolls. Secondary stress creates a central void; piercing plug opens it into a seamless shell. Then elongated/sized on a mandrel mill or pilger mill.

ERW – Electric Resistance Welding

Process: Steel strip is formed into a round shape by progressive roll forming, then edges are resistance-welded using high-frequency current. Weld bead is sheared flush. Fastest, lowest cost for line pipe <610mm OD.

DOM – Drawn Over Mandrel

Process: ERW tube is cold-drawn through a die over a mandrel. The weld zone is worked into the base metal microstructure. Produces tight OD/ID tolerances, smooth bore, and near-seamless mechanical properties.

Pilgering (Cold Rolling, Seamless)

Process: Tube shell is pushed into tapered dies that rock back and forth (pilgrim step). Reduces wall and OD in incremental strokes. Excellent wall uniformity; used for nuclear fuel rod cladding, titanium tube.

HSAW – Helical Submerged Arc

Process: Strip is continuously formed into a helix and submerged arc welded on both inside and outside. Used for large-diameter pipe (406–2540mm). The helical weld is the weakest axis under hoop stress.

Extrusion (Seamless)

Process: Heated billet is pressed through an annular die with a mandrel forming the bore. Used for stainless steel, nickel alloys, titanium, and non-ferrous metals where Mannesmann piercing is impractical.

Inspection Requirements

Hydrostatic Test

Each tube pressurized to specified test pressure for ≥5 s. Detects through-wall defects. Required per ASTM A1016 §13. Test pressure = 2S·t/OD (Barlow's formula).

Eddy Current (ET)

Coil encircles tube; induced currents disturbed by surface and near-surface defects. Per ASTM E309. Detects seams, laps, pits. Alternate to hydrostatic for small tube.

Ultrasonic (UT)

Immersion or contact transducer sends shear-wave into wall. Longitudinal and transverse notch calibration per ASTM E213. Detects wall cracks, inclusions, laminations.

Flux Leakage (MFL)

Tube magnetized; Hall sensors detect flux leakage at defects. ASTM E570. High speed (up to 2 m/s); used for carbon steel pipe and tube. Does not work on non-magnetic alloys.

Tensile Test

Transverse or longitudinal specimen per ASTM A370. Verifies UTS, YS, elongation per heat of material. Frequency: per heat/lot, not 100% inspection.

Flattening Test

Round tube compressed between parallel platens until gap = specified fraction of OD. Per ASTM A1016 §9. Checks weld quality and ductility. Tube must not crack before specified flattening.

Flare / Flange Test

Tube end expanded over a conical mandrel (flare) or flanged over a flat die. ASTM A1016 §10–11. Checks ductility and freedom from seam defects at the tube end.

Dimensional Inspection

OD checked by micrometer or laser gauge; wall by UT or micrometer; length by tape/laser. Straightness: max bow measured over full length. 100% for precision tube; sample for standard.
