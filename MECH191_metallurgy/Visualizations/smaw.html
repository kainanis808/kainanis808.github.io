Slag Removal Procedure

1
Complete the Pass
Allow bead to cool until slag has solidified (~red-to-black). Do not quench with water on hardenable steels — thermal shock can cause HAZ cracking.
2
Wear PPE
Put on face shield or goggles before chipping. Ejected slag chips are sharp and reach temperatures that cause severe burns and eye injury.
3
Chip Edges First
Strike chipping hammer at 30–45° toward bead edges. Slag typically cracks and lifts in sheets. Work along toes, then the crown.
4
Wire Brush
Brush longitudinally. Remove all loose slag, scale, and spatter down to clean, shiny metal. Any residue can become a slag inclusion in the next pass.
5
Inspect
Check for undercut, cold lap, cracks, and porosity before depositing the next pass. Use a flashlight at a low angle. Mark and grind out any defects.
6
Repeat Every Pass
Repeat steps 1–5 for each pass in a multi-pass weld. Incomplete slag removal between passes is the primary cause of slag inclusion discontinuities in SMAW.

Reconditioning Procedure

Apply when low-hydrogen electrodes have exceeded exposure limits and are within the allowed reconditioning cycle count. Never recondition cellulosic (E6010/6011) or rutile (E6013) electrodes.

1
Inspect Coating
Check for cracks, chips, or peeling. Any visible coating damage → discard immediately. Damaged coatings cannot be restored by baking.
2
Verify Cycle Count
Do not exceed the manufacturer's maximum cycles. Lincoln Electric: up to 5× for E7018; fewer for higher-strength grades. Record each cycle on the container label.
3
Load Reconditioning Oven
Place in single or double-layer configuration. Do not stack more than 3 layers — improper stacking causes uneven heat distribution and incomplete drying.
4
Set Temperature & Hold
E7018: 700 ± 25°F (370 ± 14°C) for 1 hr minimum. E8018–E10018: 700–800°F (370–425°C) for 1 hr. E11018+: consult mfr. — often 800°F (425°C). Hold time begins after oven reaches set temperature throughout the load.
5
Transfer to Storage Oven
Do not allow electrodes to cool to room temperature after reconditioning. Transfer directly to a storage oven set at 250–300°F (120–150°C).
6
Issue & Log
Issue a controlled quantity to each welder. Log electrode lot, heat number, reconditioning date, cycle count, and issuing inspector on the Low Hydrogen Control Log (AWS D1.1 §4.6.3 record requirement).
⚠ Critical Reminders (Lincoln Electric & AWS D1.1:2020)
  • Cellulosic electrodes (E6010, E6011) require controlled moisture in their coating — oven storage at elevated temperature permanently degrades arc performance. Store at ambient in dry conditions.
  • AWS D1.1 §4.6.2: Low-hydrogen electrodes must be stored at ≥ 250°F (120°C) before issue, or used within exposure limits after opening a sealed container.
  • Hydrogen-induced cracking (cold cracking / underbead cracking) can appear 24–72 hours after welding. Preheat, PWHT, and low-hydrogen procedures are the primary controls.
  • If reconditioning oven temperature or hold time cannot be verified, treat the lot as unacceptable and discard per QC procedures.
