Daniell cell (Zn | ZnSO₄ ‖ CuSO₄ | Cu) illustrating the fundamental electrochemical principles underlying galvanic corrosion. Animate to show electron and ion migration.

Anode (−) — Oxidation — Zinc

Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻  |  E° = −0.76 V (SHE)
Metal dissolves; electrons flow through external circuit toward cathode. Active (more negative) electrode always acts as anode in galvanic pair.

Cathode (+) — Reduction — Copper

Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu  |  E° = +0.34 V (SHE)
Metal deposits from solution. In open-circuit corrosion, O₂ or H⁺ is reduced instead. Cell EMF = E_cathode − E_anode = 1.10 V.

In real corrosion the anode and cathode are on the same metal surface in one electrolyte; separate half-cells shown here for instructional clarity. Cations migrate toward cathode; anions migrate toward anode through solution.
