Source: AWS D1.1/D1.1M:2020 Structural Welding Code – Steel, Clause 4 (Pre-qualified WPS), Tables 4.5, 4.4, 4.1, and Annex I. Supporting filler metal standards: AWS A5.1, A5.5 (SMAW); A5.18 (GMAW); A5.20, A5.36 (FCAW); A5.17, A5.23 (SAW).

What Are Pre-Qualified Filler Metals?

AWS D1.1 allows a Welding Procedure Specification (WPS) to be designated pre-qualified — exempt from procedure qualification testing — when it meets all requirements of Clause 4. One requirement is that only approved filler metals from Table 4.5 are used. Using a filler metal not listed in Table 4.5 requires a qualified WPS with full qualification testing.

Key rule: The filler metal must provide at least matching strength to the base metal group being welded. Under-matching is not permitted for pre-qualified WPS (except specific FCAW-S limitations noted in D1.1 Clause 4).

Pre-Qualified Base Metal Groups (D1.1 Table 4.5)

D1.1 classifies base metals into strength groups. The filler metal must match the lowest-strength base metal in the joint.

I 36 58–80 A36, A53-B, A106-B/C, A139-B, A500-A/B, A501, A516 Gr.55/65, A524, A529, A570 Gr.36–45 60 or 70 ksi (E60XX or E70XX series)
II 42–50 60–70 A572 Gr.42/50, A588 Gr.A/B/C, A606, A618, A709 Gr.36/50/50W, A852 70 ksi (E70XX, ER70S-X, E70T-X)

Group III note: A514 and A517 quenched-and-tempered steels have significant restrictions in D1.1 — heat input limits, maximum interpass temperature (400°F / 205°C), and mandatory low-hydrogen electrodes (H8 or lower). Filler metals matching Group III typically require separate AWS A5.5 or A5.28 specifications.

Process Summary

SMAW — Shielded Metal Arc
Standard: AWS A5.1 (carbon steel), A5.5 (low-alloy)
Pre-qual wires: E6010, E6011, E7014–E7048 series
Low-H required: When thickness > ¾" or Group III base metal
Positions: All (varies by electrode)
GMAW — Gas Metal Arc (MIG)
Standard: AWS A5.18 (solid & metal-cored)
Pre-qual wires: ER70S-2 through ER70S-7; E70C-3M, E70C-6M
Shielding gas: 75Ar/25CO₂ or 100% CO₂ (per wire spec)
Positions: All (varies by transfer mode)
FCAW — Flux-Cored Arc
Standard: AWS A5.20 / A5.36
Pre-qual wires: E70T-1C/M, E70T-5C/M (gas); E70T-4, E71T-8 (self)
FCAW-S limits: Restricted to specific applications; CVN may apply
Positions: All or F/H depending on wire type
SAW — Submerged Arc
Standard: AWS A5.17 (carbon steel), A5.23 (low-alloy)
Pre-qual combos: F6A-EL12, F7A0-EL12, F7A2-EM12K, etc.
Positions: Flat and horizontal only
Note: Both wire and flux classification required

SMAW Classification System (AWS A5.1)

E7018 E = Electrode  |  70 = 70,000 psi min tensile (× 1000)  |  1 = All positions (0 = F & H only)  |  8 = Flux/coating type & current (8 = low-H, DCEP or AC)

For A5.5 low-alloy electrodes, a suffix is added (e.g. E8018-C3 for 1% Ni addition). The first two digits after E are the tensile strength in ksi.

Moisture-contaminated low-hydrogen electrodes are a leading cause of hydrogen-induced cracking (HIC) in structural welds. D1.1 Clause 7.3.2 requires that all electrodes be free of oil, grease, and excessive rust on the coating.

Pre-Qualified Metal-Cored Wire (AWS A5.18)

E70C-6M E = Electrode  |  70 = 70 ksi min tensile  |  C = Composite (metal-cored, not solid)  |  6M = Chemistry; M = Ar/CO₂ mixed gas
Wire Shielding Gas Position Tensile (ksi) Key Characteristics
E70C-3M 75Ar/25CO₂ or richer Ar All 70–85 Metal-cored; higher deposition rate than solid wire; good out-of-position with spray; minimal slag
E70C-6M 75Ar/25CO₂ or richer Ar All 72–90 Most common metal-cored wire. High Si+Mn like ER70S-6; tolerates mill scale; spray or pulsed transfer; fast travel speeds
Metal-cored wires fill the tubular sheath with metallic powder (not flux). They produce little to no slag, behave like solid wire in appearance, but achieve deposition rates closer to FCAW. D1.1 requires DCEP polarity and Ar-rich shielding gas (≥75% Ar) for metal-cored wires.

SAW Classification System (AWS A5.17 — Carbon Steel)

F7A2-EM12K F = Flux  |  7 = 70 ksi min tensile of weld metal  |  A = As-welded (P = post-weld heat treated)  |  2 = CVN test temp designation (2 = −20°F; 0 = 0°F; 4 = −40°F)  |  EM12K = Wire electrode classification (E = electrode; M = Mn; 12 = C content; K = killed)

SAW requires both the flux and the electrode wire to be classified together. The complete designation (flux + wire) appears in D1.1 Table 4.5. Either the wire or the flux may be the "variable" depending on the combination tested.

F7P2-EM12K Passive (P) EM12K −20°F ≥70 Passive (neutral) flux; consistent chemistry in multi-pass; preferred when Si/Mn control is critical
F7P4-EM12K Passive (P) EM12K −40°F ≥70 Passive flux with CVN at −40°F; best for multi-pass thick-section work
Active vs Passive Flux: Active fluxes add Si and Mn to the weld metal during multi-pass welding, causing chemistry buildup that can degrade toughness in later passes. Passive (neutral) fluxes do not significantly alter weld metal chemistry — preferred for multi-pass and CVN-critical work.

Minimum Preheat & Interpass Temperature — D1.1 Table 4.4

Preheat reduces the cooling rate, minimizes hydrogen-induced cracking (HIC), and reduces residual stresses. D1.1 requires preheat when base metal thickness or carbon equivalent (CE) exceeds thresholds. Interpass temperature must not fall below the minimum preheat once welding starts.

D1.1 Rule: When base metal temperature is below 32°F (0°C), preheat to a minimum of 70°F (21°C) regardless of the values in Table 4.4.

Carbon Equivalent (CE) — Weldability Estimate

CE is used to estimate preheat requirements when not directly listed in D1.1. Higher CE = greater HIC risk = higher preheat required.
