AWS A2.4 Weld Symbol Quick Reference

The weld symbol specifies the type of weld. It is placed on the reference line: below = arrow side; above = other side. When the same symbol appears both above and below, welds are required on both sides of the joint.

Groove Weld Symbols

Square Groove
Two vertical lines; no bevel; used for thin material or full-pen with backing
V-Groove
Both members beveled; symmetric; most common full-penetration butt joint
Bevel Groove
One member beveled only; bent arrow points to prepared member
U-Groove
Both members radiused; reduces weld metal vs V-groove on thick plate
J-Groove
One member radiused; bent arrow indicates prepared member
Flare-V Groove
Two round/curved members; natural curved surface forms groove
Flare-Bevel
One flat, one round member; common in sheet metal and tube-to-plate joints

Other Weld Type Symbols

Fillet
Right-triangle symbol; T-, lap, and corner joints; no edge prep required
Plug / Slot
Rectangular symbol; hole in top member filled with weld; slot = elongated hole
Spot (Arc/Gas)
Circle below ref. line; single spot fusion weld through overlapping members
Seam (Arc/Gas)
Elongated ellipse; continuous seam weld along joint length
Back / Backing
Semicircle opposite groove symbol; back weld = deposited last; backing = first
Surfacing / Buildup
Three parallel lines; weld overlay on single surface; not a joint weld
Edge
Open rectangle; flange edges fused together; typical in sheet metal

Parts of a Weld Symbol Callout

Every weld callout is assembled from the same set of elements, each occupying a defined position on the reference line. Understanding this layout is the foundation for reading any welding drawing.

Core rule: Information below the reference line always describes the arrow side of the joint. Information above describes the other side. This applies to weld symbols, sizes, supplementary symbols, and contour modifiers alike.

Arrow Side vs. Other Side

The reference line divides the callout by which face of the joint receives the weld. The arrow physically points to the joint, identifying the arrow side member.

Fillet — Arrow Side Only

Symbol below reference line only → fillet deposited on arrow side (near face of web). The far side of the web is left unwelded.

Fillet — Both Sides

Symbol both above and below reference line → fillet welds on both sides of the web. Different leg sizes on each side are permitted.

Bent Arrow — Bevel & J-Groove Joints

For bevel and J-groove welds, only one member requires edge preparation. AWS A2.4 uses a bent (broken) arrow with the arrowhead end pointing directly at the member to be prepared, eliminating ambiguity about which piece to machine.

Rule: A bent or broken arrow indicates that the arrowhead specifically identifies the member that receives the bevel or J-groove preparation. The symbol position on the reference line (above or below) still indicates which side of the finished joint receives the weld deposit.

Surface Texture Symbols on Welded Parts

After welding, finished surfaces may require machining or grinding. Surface texture symbols per ASME Y14.36 specify roughness (Ra), waviness, and lay — placed on the drawing surface line adjacent to or combined with weld callouts.

Combining Weld Symbols with Surface Texture

Surface texture symbols are placed on the surface line of the drawing, not on the reference line. They appear adjacent to the weld symbol and indicate post-weld finishing requirements for that surface.

Flush + Grind (G) to Ra 125

Fillet weld with flush contour ground to 125 µin. Ra. Common for fatigue-critical connections and load-transfer joints where weld crown stress concentration must be eliminated.

V-Groove + Convex Contour + Machine (M)

V-groove weld with convex profile, machine finished. Used on pressure vessel weld caps where a specific crown geometry is required for inspection or service.
