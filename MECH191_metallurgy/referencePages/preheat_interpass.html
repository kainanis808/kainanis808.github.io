How the Calculator Works

The Lincoln Electric preheat model evaluates hydrogen-induced cold cracking (HIC) risk using three variables:

Variable 1 — Carbon Equivalent
CE = C + Mn/6 + (Cr+Mo+V)/5 + (Ni+Cu)/15 IIW formula (AWS D1.1 Annex I) — unitless ratio

CE quantifies how much the alloying elements increase hardenability compared to plain carbon. Higher CE = harder HAZ = greater HIC risk.

Variable 2 — Diffusible Hydrogen

Lower hydrogen filler metals allow higher CE steels or thicker sections to be welded without preheat. H4 electrodes are 4× less risky than non-LH.

Variable 3 — Section Thickness

Thicker sections cool faster (higher heat sink), creating a harder HAZ and trapping more hydrogen. D1.1 uses the thickest part at the joint, not base plate thickness alone.

CE vs Pcm — When to Use Each

IndexBest ForCarbon RangeStandard
CE (IIW) Carbon and carbon-manganese steels, mild structural steel (A36, A572) 0.16 – 0.40% C AWS D1.1 Annex I; Lincoln Electric nomograph
Pcm (Ito-Bessyo) Low-carbon micro-alloyed and high-strength steels (HSLA, A514, A572 Gr.65+) < 0.18% C JIS Z 3113; AWS D1.1 Commentary
For A514 and similar Q&T steels with C < 0.18%, Pcm often gives a better (lower) preheat estimate than CE. AWS D1.1 Annex I permits either formula; choose the one whose C range matches your steel.

Interpass Temperature Guidance

Interpass temperature is the temperature of the weld zone immediately before the next pass is deposited. Key rules:

RuleBasis
Minimum = preheat temperature. Do not let the joint cool below preheat between passes. Prevents hydrogen cracking in the HAZ of completed passes
Maximum for A514/A517 = 400°F (205°C). Allow to cool if exceeded. Prevents over-tempering of Q&T microstructure; loss of yield strength
No D1.1 maximum for Groups I/II, but Lincoln Electric recommends ≤ 550°F (290°C) for general structural carbon steel. Excessive interpass heat can reduce notch toughness (CVN) in multi-pass welds
Measure 2"–3" from the weld joint, not on the weld bead itself. D1.1 Clause 4.2.2; thermocouple or temperature crayon 50–75 mm from weld toe

Preheat Benefit by Hydrogen Level

The filler metal hydrogen level is the most powerful lever for reducing required preheat. Switching from non-LH to H4 can eliminate preheat requirements entirely on Group I/II steels up to 1½" thick.

Hydrogen Level Max H₂ (mL/100g) Typical Filler Examples Preheat Benefit vs Non-LH
H4 4.0 E7018-H4R, ER70S-6-H4, E70T-5C-H4 Eliminates preheat for all Groups I/II up to 2½"
H8 8.0 E7018-H8, E71T-1C-H8, F7A2-EM12K Reduces preheat 50–100°F vs non-LH; no preheat to ¾" for Groups I/II
H16 16.0 E7016, E7015, standard E7028 Some reduction vs non-LH; preheat still needed above ¾" for CE > 0.40
Non-LH >16 E6010, E6011, E6013 Highest preheat required; not permitted for Group III or t > ¾" per D1.1
