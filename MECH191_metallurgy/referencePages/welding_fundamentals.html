Heat Input Formula (Lincoln Electric / AWS D1.1)

HI (kJ/in) = [Voltage (V) × Current (A) × 60] / [1000 × Travel Speed (in/min)]

SMAW — Shielded Metal Arc Welding Stick / EXX

The arc is struck between a flux-coated consumable electrode and the base metal. The flux coating decomposes in the arc to generate shielding gases and forms a slag that covers the weld pool. Widely used for maintenance, structural steel, and pipeline root passes.

GMAW — Gas Metal Arc Welding MIG / Wire Feed

A continuously-fed bare solid wire electrode is melted by the arc. An externally supplied shielding gas (Ar, CO₂, or blend) protects the molten pool. Transfer mode (short-circuit, globular, spray, pulse) is controlled by voltage and wire feed speed.

GTAW — Gas Tungsten Arc Welding TIG / Heliarc

A non-consumable tungsten electrode maintains the arc. Filler metal (when required) is fed separately by hand. Argon or helium shielding gas protects the tungsten, arc, and pool. Produces the cleanest, highest-quality welds but at the lowest deposition rate.

FCAW — Flux-Cored Arc Welding Flux-Core

A tubular wire electrode with an internal flux core is continuously fed through the gun. FCAW-G uses external shielding gas; FCAW-S (self-shielded) relies solely on flux decomposition and is fully portable. Highest deposition rates of common arc processes; suited to heavy fabrication and outdoor work.

Process Diagram (FCAW-G)
FCAW-G vs FCAW-S Comparison

FCAW-G (Gas-Shielded)

  • External CO₂ or Ar/CO₂ mix required
  • Lower fume levels
  • Better bead appearance
  • Suitable for code-quality work (AWS D1.1)
  • Wind-sensitive — use windscreen
  • Common: E71T-1C, E71T-1M

FCAW-S (Self-Shielded)

  • No gas cylinder needed
  • Wind-tolerant — outdoor use
  • Higher fume — ventilate
  • Lower notch toughness typical
  • Limited to specific code applications
  • Common: E71T-8, E71T-11

Joint Types — AWS B2.1 & Miller Electric Joint Design Guide

Five basic joint configurations exist. The choice depends on load direction, accessibility, material thickness, and code requirements. Groove preparations add groove angle, root opening (gap), and root face (land) to control penetration and prevent burn-through.

Five Basic Joint Types
Butt Joint
Load: Tension, compression, bending
Code: AWS D1.1 CJP groove
T-Joint
Load: Shear along weld axis
Weld: Fillet or PJP groove
Corner Joint
Load: Tension, bending
Weld: Fillet (inside or outside)
Lap Joint
Load: Shear (eccentric)
Weld: Fillet both sides
Edge Joint
Load: Low-stress only
Weld: Edge, flange weld
Groove Weld Preparations — Dimensions (Miller Electric / AWS B2.1)

Key dimensions: Groove angle (α) — included angle between groove faces; Root opening / gap (R) — separation between members at root; Root face / land (f) — unprepared portion of base metal face.

Square Groove Butt
α: 0° (no bevel)
R: 0–3/16 in (0–5 mm)
f: Full thickness
Thickness: ≤ 3/16 in (GMAW)
Note: Requires full penetration; used for thin material
Single-V Groove
α: 60° (SMAW/FCAW), 45° (GMAW/GTAW)
R: 1/8 in (3 mm) typical
f (land): 1/16–1/8 in (1.6–3 mm)
Thickness: 3/16 in – 3/4 in
AWS D1.1: Fig. 3.3 Detail 1
Double-V Groove (X)
α: 60° each side (30° each bevel)
R: 0–1/8 in
f: 1/8 in land
Thickness: >3/4 in — saves filler vs single-V
Note: Access from both sides required
Single-Bevel Groove
α: 45° bevel (one side only)
R: 1/16–1/8 in
f: 1/16–3/32 in
Thickness: 3/16 – 3/4 in
Note: One-sided access; T-joints common
Single-J Groove
α: 10–20° bevel angle
R: 1/8 in typical
f: 1/8 in land
Root radius: 3/8 in
Note: Less filler than V; needs machining
Fillet Weld (T or Lap)
Throat: 0.707 × leg (45° fillet)
Min. leg (per AWS D1.1 Table 5.8):
  ≤1/4 in → 1/8 in fillet
  1/4–1/2 in → 3/16 in fillet
  1/2–3/4 in → 1/4 in fillet
  >3/4 in → 5/16 in fillet
Max. leg: ≤ plate thickness (at edge)

Preheat guidance (AWS D1.1 Table 4.2): For carbon steel: Carbon Equivalent CE = C + Mn/6 + (Cr+Mo+V)/5 + (Ni+Cu)/15.
