Units and Topics

This is a simple page containing the projected units and topics of MECH 191.

Unit 1: Foundations of Material Science and Metallography

  • Atomic structure, bonding, and physical/chemical properties of metals (density, melting point, thermal and electrical conductivity, magnetic behavior)
  • Crystal lattices (BCC, FCC, HCP) and the link between structure and property
  • Crystal defects, dislocations, grain boundaries, and ASTM grain size
  • Metallography workflow: sectioning, mounting, grinding, polishing, and etching (ASTM E3, E407)
  • Demo 1: Metallography Sample Preparation + Optical Microscopy (PPE required)
  • Unit 1 Quiz

Unit 2: Phase Diagrams and Alloy Systems

  • Phase equilibria and binary phase diagrams (eutectic, eutectoid, peritectic — conceptual reading)
  • Lever rule (chart-reading approach, no derivations)
  • Iron-carbon system: hypoeutectoid, eutectoid, and hypereutectoid steels; cast iron region
  • Demo 2: Phase Diagrams Workshop (Lever Rule + Fe-C Diagrams)
  • Ferrous alloy classification: AISI/SAE numbering, plain carbon and alloy steels, stainless grades (austenitic, ferritic, martensitic), tool steels, cast iron types (gray, ductile, white, malleable)
  • Non-ferrous alloy systems: aluminum, copper, titanium, nickel, magnesium families
  • Reading mill test reports and material certifications
  • Demo 3: Material Identification in the Field — spark testing and introduction to PMI / portable XRF
  • Unit 2 Quiz

Unit 3: Mechanical Properties, Testing, and Non-Destructive Testing

  • Stress, strain, and the tensile curve (reading, not deriving)
  • Strengthening mechanisms explained conceptually (cold work, alloying, grain refinement, heat treatment)
  • Demo 4: Tensile Testing (ASTM E8)
  • Hardness testing methods and scale conversions (Brinell, Rockwell, Vickers, Knoop)
  • Impact testing and ductile-to-brittle transition
  • Demo 5: Hardness and Impact Testing (ASTM E18, E10, E92, E23)
  • Fatigue and creep — practical signatures and where they appear in service
  • Introduction to NDT: dye penetrant, magnetic particle, ultrasonic, eddy current, radiographic overview
  • Demo 6: NDT Methods — hands-on dye penetrant and magnetic particle inspection
  • Unit 3 Quiz

Unit 4: Heat Treatment, Welding, and Manufacturing Processes

  • TTT and CCT diagrams — reading cooling curves and predicting microstructure
  • In-class TTT/CCT interpretation workshop using pre-made quenched sample library
  • Annealing, normalizing, stress relief, and quenching (media selection, hardenability)
  • Demo 7: Heat Treatment of Steel — student-run anneal, quench, and temper
  • Tempering, surface hardening (carburizing, nitriding, induction, flame)
  • Microstructure analysis after heat treatment
  • Demo 8: Post-Treatment Microstructure Analysis — hardness and microstructure comparison of Demo 7 samples
  • Welding metallurgy essentials: heat-affected zone formation, cracking modes, why post-weld heat treatment exists
  • Manufacturing processes (casting, forming, machining, powder metallurgy, additive) and intro to material selection
  • Unit 4 Quiz

Unit 5: Corrosion, Failure Analysis, and Material Selection

  • Corrosion mechanisms: uniform, galvanic, pitting, crevice, intergranular, stress corrosion, erosion
  • Protection strategies: coatings, cathodic protection, inhibitors, material selection
  • Demo 9: Galvanic Corrosion (setup in Week 13, observations in Week 14)
  • Methods in failure analysis: investigation workflow, root cause analysis
  • Reading fracture surfaces: ductile cup-and-cone, brittle cleavage, fatigue beach marks, intergranular
  • Demo 10: Fractography using a prepared fracture specimen library
  • Material selection checklist for technicians (cost, strength, environment, manufacturability, sustainability)
  • Unit 5 Quiz
